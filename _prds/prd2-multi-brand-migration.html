Migrating a Multi-Brand Insurer to Full Platform Parity

TL;DR

Enable Tractable's Estimating and Total-Loss Triage use case for one of Europe's largest motor insurers and its digital-first subsidiary brand, who share a single legacy claims stack. This PRD scopes the migration to full feature parity across both brands, plus a licence-plate matching capability requested as part of the move.

1. Introduction

Goal: Migrate both the client and its digital-first subsidiary off their shared legacy claims stack onto Tractable's current platform, with full feature parity for each brand and the licence-plate matching capability they've requested as part of the move.

Scope: The feature-parity work for both brands, the claim-handler and policyholder workflows, the case-status lifecycle, and the new VRM/licence-plate matching capability. A broader document-verification package beyond licence plates (grey card, VIN-document checks) is tracked separately and out of scope here.

2. Background

Customer Profile

The client is one of its country's largest motor insurers, operating alongside a digital-first subsidiary brand that targets a younger, app-native customer base. Both brands share a single backend and claims stack, though each has its own customer-facing capture experience.

The Use Case: Estimating & Total-Loss Triage

The client uses Tractable's Estimating use case to generate an AI repair estimate and a Total-Loss eligibility verdict at the moment of intake, so claim handlers can move straight to a cash-settlement decision. Eligible claims are triaged automatically; the rest are routed back for manual handling.

3. System Specifications

3.1 FNOL Input Portal for Claim Handlers
When a policyholder contacts either brand, claim handlers use this portal to capture initial claim data and send it to the client's backend, which runs an initial eligibility check to determine whether the case qualifies for Tractable processing.
3.2 Client Backend
Triages initial claim data for eligibility, then sends the vehicle's VIN and accident date to Tractable in exchange for a claim-specific link. Delivers that link to the policyholder to start the photo-capture journey, then ingests the AI-processed results back via API or portal integration to finalise the claim.
3.3 Tractable Backend
Generates the claim-specific link on request, then calculates the repair estimate and Total-Loss eligibility once the policyholder completes capture. Makes both available through three channels: API, email, and the portal.
3.4 Tractable Input Capture App
Guides the policyholder through a sequence of prompts to capture the required vehicle images, with secure transmission to the Tractable backend for automated AI processing.
3.5 Subsidiary's Input Capture App
A brand-specific variant of the Input Capture App for the digital subsidiary, with its own visual styling and an auto-redirect step once the policyholder finishes the flow.

5. Pain Points & Areas of Improvement

Portal
No clear visibility into why a claim is marked out of scope, and the capture link expires after three days, forcing users to start a brand-new claim submission under the same claim ID just to regenerate it.
Capture App
Users have to tap multiple times to capture photos within the same bucket instead of doing it in one continuous flow, and there's no clear feedback explaining why they can't proceed when the minimum image requirement isn't met.

6. AI Assessment & Configuration Rules

Out-of-Scope Reasons
Every claim that doesn't qualify for automated processing is tagged with a specific out-of-scope reason. The full reason-code list wasn't finalised in the source document; tracked internally rather than reproduced here.
Licence-Plate (VRM) Matching
Runs whenever a claim has both a registration number and at least one four-corner photo, classifying the result as Matched, Not Matched, or Unable to Assess (occluded, blurry, or no plate visible). If the plate-recognition call itself fails, the system treats it as Unable to Assess rather than blocking the estimate. Surfaced as a label in the portal and claim-handler emails today; configurable per brand to also force an out-of-scope decision when the plates don't match.

7. Claim Status Lifecycle & Constraints

Link ExpiredLink Expired
The Input Capture App link expired before all information was received. Expiry is configurable (default three days); if the policyholder already submitted some information, they resume from their last completed step on the new link.
ProcessingProcessing
Tractable is processing the claim.
Ready For ReviewReady For Review
The Tractable estimate is ready for the claim handler to review.
FailedUnable To Process
Tractable processing failed. Surfaced to Level 1 Support and the CSM team to debug the failure.
